### GAP 2 — HABIT FORMATION & BEHAVIORAL DESIGN
**Status:** ❌ Belum ada agent yang ditugaskan  
**Priority:** 🔴 HIGH  
**Topik yang hilang:**
- Habit loop theory (Duhigg 2012; Wood & Neal 2007) — cue-routine-reward
- Implementation intentions (Gollwitzer 1999, 2006) — "I will study Japanese AT [time] IN [place]"
- Habit formation timeline research (Lally et al. 2010 — 66 days median, range 18–254 days)
- Variable ratio reinforcement (Skinner tradition) — psychological basis of streak mechanics
- Loss aversion (Kahneman & Tversky 1979) — why streak-break anxiety works
- Commitment devices (Ariely & Wertenbroch 2002) — behavioral economics angle for daily goals
- Temptation bundling (Milkman et al. 2014) — pairing study with enjoyable activity

**Why this gap is critical:** Setiap fitur Nugget Nihongo yang dirancang untuk membentuk kebiasaan belajar harian (streak, reminder, daily goal) perlu justifikasi behavioral science. Ini BERBEDA dari gamification — ini adalah science of habit formation, bukan reward mechanics.

**Suggested section:** §11 atau digabung dengan §10 sebagai §10.6–§10.7  
**Suggested agent:** Agent 6 (bisa dibundel dengan gamification; keduanya behavioral psychology)  
**Citation seeds:**

### GAP 3 — PHONOLOGY & PRONUNCIATION LEARNING
**Status:** ❌ Tidak ada coverage yang memadai  
**Priority:** 🔴 HIGH  
**What's currently in corpus:**  
- §5.5 covers: mora timing error rates (6%/11%/18%), pitch accent, sokuon/chōon interference — deskriptif, bukan learning science
- §9.7 covers: audio-visual integration for comprehension — bukan pronunciation acquisition
- Compass §3 covers: data source licensing untuk pitch accent (WaDoku, Kanjium) — bukan pedagogy

**Topik yang hilang (learning science):**
- Speech Learning Model / SLM (Flege 1995, 1999) — bagaimana L2 phoneme dipelajari relatif terhadap L1 phoneme inventory
- Perceptual Assimilation Model / PAM (Best 1995; Best & Tyler 2007) — bagaimana learner mengasimilasi L2 suara ke L1 kategori
- Mora perception oleh learner L1 stress-timed (Cutler & Otake 1994; McQueen et al. 2001)
- Pitch accent acquisition research (khususnya untuk pelajar non-tonal L1)
- High Variability Phonetic Training / HVPT (Logan et al. 1991; Bradlow et al. 1997) — variasi talker dalam audio training
- Phonological neighborhood effects di L2 lexical access (Vitevitch & Rodríguez 2005)
- Kanji reading pronunciation acquisition (kunyomi vs. onyomi pattern learning)

**Why this gap is critical:** Aplikasi bahasa yang mengklaim evidence-based tetapi tidak memiliki phonological learning theory untuk audio features-nya memiliki celah akademis besar. Terutama karena pitch accent adalah unique challenge untuk learner Indonesia.

**Suggested section:** §11 — Phonology & Pronunciation Learning  
**Suggested agent:** Agent 5 (new assignment needed)  
**Estimated citations:** 40–60

### GAP 4 — OUTPUT THEORY & NOTICING HYPOTHESIS
**Status:** ❌ Coverage sangat minimal  
**Priority:** 🟠 HIGH  
**What's currently in corpus:**
- Krashen Input Hypothesis ada di §1 (CI cluster) — dengan catatan "sufficient" overstated
- Long (1996) Interaction Hypothesis disebutkan di compass tapi tidak expanded
- Tidak ada Swain, tidak ada Schmidt

**Topik yang hilang:**
- Swain's Output Hypothesis (1985, 1995, 2005) — speaking/writing forces noticing of gaps
- Schmidt's Noticing Hypothesis (1990, 1993) — conscious awareness required for acquisition
- Focus on Form vs. Focus on FormS (Long 1991; Doughty & Williams 1998)
- Output and automaticity (DeKeyser 2001, 2007) — skill acquisition theory
- The role of production in vocabulary consolidation (Barcroft 2002, 2004)
- Pushed output and explicit awareness (Izumi 2002)

**Why this gap is critical:** §1 justifies kenapa Nugget Nihongo tests (not just restudies), tapi tidak ada theoretical framework yang secara eksplisit justify kenapa *production* (not just recognition) matters. Output theory provides this. Tanpa ini, methodology page terlihat mengabaikan half of SLA theory.

**Suggested section:** §12 atau §1 addendum  
**Suggested agent:** Agent 4 (new assignment needed)  
**Estimated citations:** 25–35

### GAP 5 — INDIVIDUAL DIFFERENCES & LEARNER VARIABLES
**Status:** ❌ Scattered mentions, no dedicated section  
**Priority:** 🟠 HIGH  
**What's currently in corpus:**
- §8 malu/face section covers anxiety dari cross-cultural angle (CC cluster)
- §CA.8 covers learner autonomy briefly
- §9.4 expertise reversal covers level differences tapi bukan individual differences

**Topik yang hilang:**
- Foreign Language Classroom Anxiety Scale / FLCA (Horwitz, Horwitz & Cope 1986) — paling relevan: input anxiety, processing anxiety, output anxiety
- Language Learning Aptitude (Carroll 1981; Skehan 1998) — phonetic coding ability, inductive ability, memory
- LLAMA test (Meara 2005) — modern aptitude assessment
- Working memory capacity and L2 learning (Miyake & Friedman 1998; Juffs & Harrington 2011)
- Age effects / Critical Period Hypothesis (Lenneberg 1967; Long 1990; Birdsong & Molis 2001)
- Growth mindset × language learning (Dweck 2006; Mercer & Ryan 2010)
- Attribution theory (Weiner 1985) — failure attribution: luck vs. ability vs. effort

**Why this gap is critical:** FSRS treats all learners the same algorithmically, but individual differences determine hugely whether the system works. Especially anxiety — which §8 covers cross-culturally but not psychometrically. FLCA is the most-cited construct in language learning psychology.

**Suggested section:** §13  
**Suggested agent:** Agent 3 (new assignment needed)  
**Estimated citations:** 40–50

### GAP 6 — SLEEP, MEMORY CONSOLIDATION & STUDY TIMING
**Status:** ❌ Not in corpus at all  
**Priority:** 🟡 MEDIUM  
**Topik yang hilang:**
- Sleep-dependent memory consolidation (Walker & Stickgold 2004; Diekelmann & Born 2010)
- Targeted Memory Reactivation / TMR during sleep (Rudoy et al. 2009)
- Nap studies for L2 vocabulary (Gais et al. 2006; Korman et al. 2007)
- Optimal time-of-day for encoding vs. retrieval (circadian research — Folkard & Monk 1985)
- Post-learning interference effects — studying two vocab lists back-to-back
- SRS scheduling implications: should reviews be avoided immediately post-encoding or immediately pre-sleep?

**Why this gap matters:** SRS is fundamentally about timing. The sleep/consolidation literature directly extends the SRS rationale — spacing works partly because sleep consolidation happens between sessions. §1 covers spacing extensively but has zero mention of the neurobiological mechanism (sleep). This is a credibility gap.

**Suggested section:** §1 addendum atau §14  
**Suggested agent:** Could be bundled with Agent 6 OR separate agent  
**Estimated citations:** 20–30

### GAP 7 — VOCABULARY KNOWLEDGE DEPTH & WORD KNOWLEDGE DIMENSIONS
**Status:** ❌ Minimal coverage  
**Priority:** 🟡 MEDIUM  
**What's currently in corpus:**
- Nation's Five Criteria (selection) in §CA.1 ✅
- Nation's Vocabulary Levels Test mentioned in §CA.7 briefly
- Breadth vs. depth distinction not fully developed

**Topik yang hilang:**
- Read's (2000) vocabulary knowledge depth model — 9 aspects of word knowledge
- Word Associates Test / WAT (Read 1993, 1998) — measures depth
- Vocabulary Knowledge Scale / VKS (Wesche & Paribakht 1996) — mentioned in §CA.7 but not expanded
- Schmidt's incidental vs. intentional vocabulary learning (1994, 2000)
- Schmitt's (2010) incremental acquisition model — knowing a word is not binary
- Nation's Vocabulary Levels Test (1983, 1990, 2001) — diagnostic tool
- Word frequency bands and optimal coverage (Nation 2006: 95% coverage needs 3,000 word families)

**Why this gap matters:** Nugget Nihongo cards presumably teach multiple aspects of a word (reading, meaning, kanji, usage). Without vocabulary depth theory, there's no principled answer to: "how many card types should one word have?" or "when does a learner 'know' a word?"

**Suggested section:** §CA addendum atau §15  
**Suggested agent:** Could be §CA addendum for Agent 9, or new agent  
**Estimated citations:** 20–30

### GAP 8 — ERROR ANALYSIS & INTERLANGUAGE THEORY
**Status:** ❌ Not in corpus  
**Priority:** 🟡 MEDIUM  
**What's currently in corpus:**
- §5.5 covers interference points (contrastive analysis) — predicts errors
- §CA covers CALL and washback — mentions assessment of errors implicitly
- No Corder, no Selinker, no interlanguage theory

**Topik yang hilang:**
- Corder (1967) error analysis — errors are evidence of learning, not failure
- Selinker (1972) interlanguage — learner language as a legitimate linguistic system
- Fossilization (Selinker & Lamendella 1979; Han 2004)
- Error typology: transfer errors, developmental errors, simplification errors, induced errors
- The role of error feedback in SRS: immediate vs. delayed correction
- Computer-Assisted Error Analysis (CAEA) tools

**Why this gap matters:** Error analysis is the bridge between §5.5 (what errors Indonesian learners make) and §1 (how retrieval practice corrects them). Without this bridge, the error → correction → learning loop has no theoretical foundation. Also relevant for Juicy agent: which errors deserve explicit L2d notes?

**Suggested section:** §5.5 addendum atau §16  
**Suggested agent:** Agent 11 addendum OR new agent  
**Estimated citations:** 20–25
