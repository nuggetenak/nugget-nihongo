## CONTEXT: POSISI DALAM CORPUS

**§CA sudah ada** (Agent 9 v1, 86 citations) mencakup vocabulary *breadth*: Nation's word frequency lists (1990, 2001, 2007, 2013), receptive vs. productive vocabulary distinction, CEFR-J/JLPT sequencing, needs analysis, curriculum architecture, CALL, assessment.

**Gap yang tersisa** adalah vocabulary *depth*: mengetahui sebuah kata bukan hanya "kenal artinya" tetapi memahami collocations, morphological family, word associations, register, dan usage context. Depth theory adalah justifikasi teoritis untuk mengapa Nugget Nihongo cards yang include contoh kalimat, collocational notes, dan usage context lebih efektif dari simple definition-only cards.

**Catatan penting:** §12 (Agent 4) sudah menyentuh Webb (2005) sebagai OT-28 dari sudut pandang *receptive vs. productive practice*. §CA addendum ini mengangkat Webb dan literatur depth dari sudut pandang *knowledge representation* — berbeda dan complementary. Webb (2008, 2009) belum ada di corpus — tambahkan jika relevan, tapi jangan re-cite Webb (2005) sebagai entry baru (sudah OT-28).

## SCOPE & SUBSECTIONS YANG DIPERLUKAN

### §CA.10.1 — Depth vs. Breadth Distinction
**Core question:** Mengapa knowing 5000 words "at surface level" tidak sama dengan knowing 2000 words deeply?

**Citation seeds:**
- Read, J. (2004). Plumbing the depths: How should the construct of vocabulary knowledge be defined? In Bogaards & Laufer (Eds.), *Vocabulary in a second language* (pp. 209–227). Benjamins. *(no DOI — book chapter)*
- Schmitt, N. (2014). Size and depth of vocabulary knowledge: What the research shows. *Language Learning, 64*(4), 913–951. https://doi.org/10.1111/lang.12077
- Qian, D. D. (1999). Assessing the roles of depth and breadth of vocabulary knowledge in reading comprehension. *Canadian Modern Language Review, 56*(2), 282–307. https://doi.org/10.3138/cmlr.56.2.282

**Klaim kunci:** Breadth dan depth adalah konstruk yang berbeda secara psikologis. SRS yang hanya tests definition → recognition builds breadth only. Cards dengan contextual information build depth.

---

### §CA.10.2 — Vocabulary Knowledge Scale (VKS) & Lexical Profiles
**Core question:** Bagaimana kita mendeskripsikan tahapan pengetahuan kosakata?

**Citation seeds:**
- Paribakht, T. S., & Wesche, M. (1997). Vocabulary enhancement activities and reading for meaning in second language vocabulary acquisition. In Coady & Huckin (Eds.), *Second language vocabulary acquisition* (pp. 174–202). Cambridge University Press.
- Laufer, B. (1998). The development of passive and active vocabulary in a second language: Same or different? *Applied Linguistics, 19*(2), 255–271. https://doi.org/10.1093/applin/19.2.255
- Meara, P. (1996). The dimensions of lexical competence. In Brown et al. (Eds.), *Performance and competence in second language acquisition* (pp. 35–53). Cambridge University Press.

**Klaim kunci:** Pengetahuan kosakata progresif: unfamiliar → recognized but not known → partial knowledge → full productive mastery. SRS confidence rating (Again/Hard/Good/Easy dalam FSRS) dapat di-mapped ke tahapan ini.

---

### §CA.10.3 — Collocational Knowledge
**Core question:** Mengapa pelajar yang "tahu" sebuah kata tetap salah menggunakannya dalam kalimat?

**Citation seeds:**
- Howarth, P. (1998). Phraseology and second language proficiency. *Applied Linguistics, 19*(1), 24–44. https://doi.org/10.1093/applin/19.1.24
- Nesselhauf, N. (2005). *Collocations in a learner corpus.* John Benjamins. *(no DOI — monograph)*
- Wray, A. (2002). *Formulaic language and the lexicon.* Cambridge University Press. *(no DOI — monograph)*

**Japanese application:** Japanese する verbs (勉強する, 説明する) dan を collocations memerlukan explicit collocation knowledge yang tidak dapat diperoleh dari definition-only cards. Kartu yang menyertakan contoh kalimat dengan collocational partner adalah depth-building intervention yang terdokumentasi.

---

### §CA.10.4 — Morphological Awareness & Kanji Compound Networks
**Core question:** Bagaimana pengetahuan komponen kanji mempercepat akuisisi kosakata baru?

**Citation seeds:**
- Kieffer, M. J., & Lesaux, N. K. (2008). The role of derivational morphology in the reading comprehension of Spanish-speaking English language learners. *Reading and Writing, 21*(8), 783–804. https://doi.org/10.1007/s11145-007-9078-2
- Bauer, L., & Nation, I. S. P. (1993). Word families. *International Journal of Lexicography, 6*(4), 253–279. https://doi.org/10.1093/ijl/6.4.253
- Cai, M. (2015). The effects of learning kanji compounds through morphological analysis on vocabulary size. *Journal of the Japanese Language Teaching Association, 162*, 32–47. *(cari DOI atau JJLTA-equivalent)*

**Japanese application:** Knowing 水 (water) + 泳 (swim) predicts 水泳 (swimming). Kanji compound morphology adalah partially transparent network — morphological awareness training dapat accelerate depth acquisition signifikan untuk Indonesian learners yang mulai dari zero kanji knowledge.

---

### §CA.10.5 — Word Association Networks & Semantic Depth
**Core question:** Bagaimana koneksi asosiatif antara kata menentukan depth of knowledge?

**Citation seeds:**
- Meara, P., & Wolter, B. (2004). V_Links: Beyond vocabulary depth. *Angles on the English Speaking World, 4*, 85–96.
- Wolter, B. (2001). Comparing the L1 and L2 mental lexicon: A depth of individual word knowledge model. *Studies in Second Language Acquisition, 23*(1), 41–69. https://doi.org/10.1017/S0272263101001024
- Aitchison, J. (2003). *Words in the mind: An introduction to the mental lexicon* (3rd ed.). Blackwell. *(no DOI — monograph)*

**Design implication:** Deck yang mengelompokkan kata berdasarkan semantic networks (bukan hanya JLPT level) builds deeper associative connections — justified oleh word association research.

### §CA.10.6 — Design Implications: Depth-Building Card Features
Sintesis §CA.10.1–CA.10.5 → spesifikasi actionable:
1. Contoh kalimat wajib (bukan opsional) untuk kata dengan collocational behavior (verbs, adverbs)
2. Morphological family notes untuk kanji compounds
3. Register/usage notes (formal vs. informal register untuk kata seperti めし vs ご飯)
4. Cross-card links untuk semantic network groups
